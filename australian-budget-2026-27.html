Budget Measures
Key Announcements

The 2026–27 budget prioritises tax relief, fuel security, housing, hospitals and defence amid a global energy shock.

💰
$250
Working Australians Tax Offset
New annual tax offset for 13+ million workers from 1 July 2027. Additional tax cuts legislated for 2026 and 2027.
🏥
$25B
Record Hospital Investment
Additional investment in public hospitals under the National Health Reform Agreement, including $15.9B pre-provisioned.
🛡️
$53B
Record Defence Spending
Record defence investment including the 2026 National Defence Strategy and Integrated Investment Program for military capability.
⛽
20%
Gas Export Reservation
20% of gas exports reserved for domestic use. Fuel excise temporarily reduced in response to Middle East oil shock driving 40–70% price surges.
🏠
65,000
New Homes Infrastructure
Funding for infrastructure to enable up to 65,000 new homes. 75,000 additional Australians supported into home ownership over the decade via tax reform.
🚂
$8.6B
Road & Rail Projects
Significant infrastructure investment, plus $8.6B in housing infrastructure funding to support new home building.
📋
$10.2B
Regulatory Burden Reduction
Annual reduction in business regulatory burden. $3.5B in new business tax cuts. $540M/yr in compliance cost savings for businesses and individuals.
♿
$184.9B
NDIS Reform Savings
Major NDIS structural reform: scheme growth reset to ~2% during transition period (was running at higher rates), then returning to 5% from 2030–31. Saves $184.9B over 12 years.
