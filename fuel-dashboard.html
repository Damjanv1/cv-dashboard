§5

Is it worth the drive?

Net savings after fuel burned on the detour

Enter a postcode or address in the panel above, set your car details, then click Calculate.

How is the net saving calculated?
For every station within your max drive radius we work out:
  1. Drive distance = straight-line × 1.3 (the road-factor fudge for real roads vs crow-flies).
  2. Round-trip fuel burnt = (2 × drive km) ÷ 100 × your car's L/100 km.
  3. Fuel cost of the detour = litres burnt × your local baseline price (conservative).
  4. Time cost = (round-trip ÷ 50 km/h) × your $/hr, if set.
  5. Savings = (baseline − station price) × your typical fill size.
  6. Net = Savings − Fuel cost − Time cost.
The baseline is the median price of stations within 3 km of your location (fallback 5 km). A station only counts as "worth the drive" if Net > $0.50 per fill. Distances are not real turn-by-turn routes — Phase 2 of this project will wire in actual driving directions for higher accuracy.
